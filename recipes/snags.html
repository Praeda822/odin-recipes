Aussie BBQ Snags

Barbecue snags are a type of Australian sausage, generally prepared using beef, pork, garlic, and onions.

Normally thick in texture with a mild flavour, barbecue snags are usually served along with mashed potatoes, grilled onions, tomato sauce, barbecue sauce, chutneys and loaves of artisanal bread on the side.

25 minutes
Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1kg Beef Sausages
  • 1 loaf White Bread (1 slice per snag)
  • 2 White Onions
  • BBQ or tomato sauce
  • Canola Oil

Steps

  1. Begin by taking the onions and slicing them. Now, take a pan and put two tablespoons of oil in it. Over medium heat, saut√© the onions and cook until they turn golden brown in colour. Once done, keep the pan aside.
  2. Now, take another pan and heat two tablespoons of oil in it. Add the sausages to the oil and fry them. Cook until they are browned throughout. Make sure you flip the sausages from time to time. This will allow them to cook evenly from all sides.
  3. When you are almost done, carefully slice all the sausages through the middle to butterfly them. Now turn them over, allowing the insides to cook properly as well.
  4. After the sausages reach your preferred level of doneness, it is time for plating. Take a sausage and place it diagonally on a slice of white bread. Add sauteed onions to the top, and drizzle with either tomato or barbecue sauce.
  5. Repeat for the rest of the sausages, and there you have it! Your delicious barbequed snags are ready to be served!
